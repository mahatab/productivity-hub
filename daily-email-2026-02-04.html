📖 TODAY'S READING

Meditations by Marcus Aurelius (Book II)

Today's passage from the great Stoic philosopher and Roman Emperor reminds us about character and virtue. Marcus Aurelius, despite being one of the most powerful men in the world, lived with remarkable simplicity. He was "trained to hardihood by wrestling, hunting, and outdoor games" and "kept from the extravagancies of his day."

💭 Key Insight: Marcus learned from his grandfather "to be gentle and meek, and to refrain from all anger and passion." Even Emperor Hadrian recognized his truthfulness, calling him "Verissimus" - more truthful than his own name.

🎯 For Today: True strength isn't about power or wealth - it's about character, self-discipline, and staying true to your values even when the world offers you everything else.
